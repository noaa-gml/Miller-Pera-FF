Model input, code, and output in support of gridded fossil-fuel CO₂ emission estimates for atmospheric inversions

The datasets linked here are in support of:

Gridded 1° × 1° monthly fossil-fuel CO₂ emission estimates (1993–2025) for use as fluxes in atmospheric inversion systems. Version 2026 (March 2026). Pera, Jacobson, and Miller, NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory.

This product combines national inventories from CDIAC at AppState, the Energy Institute Statistical Review, and USGS Mineral Commodity Summaries with spatial patterns from EDGAR v8.0 to produce monthly gridded emissions on a global 1° × 1° grid for fossil fuel emissions broken down by CDIAC categories: solid/coal, liquid/petroleum, gas/natural gas, flaring, cement, and bunker fuels.

A near-real-time companion product, v2026b, extends the time series through April 2026 using CarbonMonitor for inversion runs that can’t wait for the next Energy Institute release. See the v2026b NRT extension section of the methodology for details.

Data files and model code are contained in the following directories:

code/

Python pipeline for ingesting and harmonizing inventories, gridding national emissions to 1° × 1° cells, extrapolating beyond the CDIAC record, interpolating annual totals to monthly fields, and reformatting output for CarbonTracker delivery. Also includes a Jupyter verification notebook with 90+ automated checks and a README with full methodology documentation.

outputs/

Monthly, gridded, 1° × 1° fossil-fuel CO₂ flux fields in CF-compliant netCDF format:

  • gml_ff_co2_2026.nc — Monolithic file containing all years (1993–2025) in a single dataset.
  • ct/ — CarbonTracker-format files: 33 per-year files and 396 per-month files for direct use in inversion systems.
  • summary_figure.png — Overview figure shown above.

Input data sources

Input data are not included in this archive due to size and licensing constraints. All sources are publicly available from their respective providers:

  • CDIAC at AppState (Erb & Marland, 2026) — National and global annual fossil-fuel CO₂ emissions by fuel type (solid, liquid, gas, cement, flaring, bunker fuels), 1751–2023. Available at https://rieee.appstate.edu/projects-programs/cdiac/
  • Energy Institute Statistical Review of World Energy (2025 edition) — Year-over-year production changes by fuel type and by country, used to extrapolate national emissions beyond the CDIAC record (2024–2025). Available at https://www.energyinst.org/statistical-review
  • EDGAR v8.0 (Crippa et al., 2024) — Gridded 0.1° × 0.1° sector-specific emission maps (combustion, cement, flaring) providing the spatial patterns used to distribute national totals onto the 1° × 1° grid. Available at https://edgar.jrc.ec.europa.eu/dataset_ghg2025
  • USGS Mineral Commodity Summaries — Cement — National cement production statistics used to extrapolate cement emissions beyond the CDIAC record. Available at https://www.usgs.gov/centers/national-minerals-information-center/cement-statistics-and-information
  • GISS Country Grid — 1° × 1° country assignment grid used to map national totals to grid cells. Available at https://data.giss.nasa.gov/landuse/country.html
  • Seasonal cycle factors — North American monthly factors from Blasing et al. (2005) and EDGAR-derived Eurasian seasonal cycles, used to modulate annual fields to monthly resolution.

Download locations and file-naming conventions are documented in the README included with the code.
